# Privacy Policy for EYFS CRM Custom GPT

**Last updated:** 10 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the EYFS CRM Custom GPT and its connected action endpoint handle information submitted by authorized users.

This policy applies only to the Custom GPT and its connected workflow used to support business development CRM activities. It is not a public EY service, public customer-facing product, or replacement for any applicable enterprise privacy notice, internal policy, or client confidentiality obligation.

## 1. Purpose of the Custom GPT

The EYFS CRM Custom GPT is designed to help authorized users capture, structure, query, and manage business development information, including client interactions, opportunities, action items, follow-ups, relationship context, data quality issues, and related CRM records.

The GPT may interact with a connected backend service, such as a Google Apps Script endpoint and Google Sheets-based CRM workbook, to log updates, retrieve CRM information, and support controlled workflow actions.

## 2. Information processed

Depending on what the user submits, the Custom GPT and connected action may process the following types of information:

- Business development notes and meeting summaries
- Client or prospect organization names
- Names, roles, and business contact context for stakeholders
- Opportunity descriptions, next steps, and status updates
- Action items, due dates, owners, and follow-up notes
- Data quality issues and remediation updates
- System metadata such as timestamps, request IDs, audit records, and processing status

Users should not submit unnecessary sensitive personal information, personal financial information, personal health information, credentials, passwords, secrets, or confidential information that is not required for the CRM workflow.

## 3. How information is used

Information submitted through the Custom GPT may be used to:

- Capture original user inputs for traceability
- Create or update structured CRM records
- Query CRM status, pipeline, actions, asks, follow-ups, and relationship information
- Generate operational summaries and dashboards
- Maintain audit logs, data quality logs, and change history
- Support workflow controls such as action closure, follow-up updates, and data quality remediation

The Custom GPT and connected backend are intended to support internal business development productivity and CRM discipline.

## 4. Where information is stored

Information may be stored in the connected CRM data store, including Google Sheets or related Google Apps Script-managed logs, depending on the configured implementation.

The connected backend may store:

- Raw intake records
- Structured CRM records
- Audit logs
- Field change logs
- Data quality logs
- Action and remediation history

Information is retained for operational traceability unless deleted or archived according to the owner’s applicable internal procedures.

## 5. Access and sharing

Access to the CRM backend and related Google assets should be limited to authorized users only.

The Custom GPT may be shared with selected users or by link, depending on the configuration selected by the GPT owner and the applicable ChatGPT workspace settings.

Users with access to the GPT may be able to submit information to the connected action endpoint. The backend may process those submissions according to the permissions and controls configured by the GPT owner.

## 6. Third-party services

The Custom GPT may use third-party services necessary to operate the workflow, including:

- OpenAI / ChatGPT for GPT interaction and action invocation
- Google Apps Script for backend workflow execution
- Google Sheets for CRM data storage
- GitHub Pages for hosting this privacy policy page

Information sent through the GPT action may be processed by the connected backend service. Users should ensure they are authorized to submit any information they provide.

## 7. User responsibilities

Users are responsible for ensuring that information submitted to the GPT is appropriate, accurate, and permitted for the intended CRM purpose.

Users should not submit:

- Passwords or authentication secrets
- Unnecessary personal data
- Highly sensitive personal information
- Confidential client information unless authorized
- Information that violates applicable internal policies, contractual obligations, or laws

## 8. Security and controls

The CRM workflow is designed to support controlled processing, including traceability, audit logging, and structured change history.

Where implemented, operational controls may include:

- Request logging
- Change request tracking
- Field-level change logs
- Audit logs
- Data quality validation
- Controlled frontend write-back
- Versioned or append-only record updates

These controls are intended to reduce the risk of accidental data loss and improve accountability.

## 9. Data retention

CRM records, audit logs, and related operational records may be retained as needed for business development tracking, operational continuity, and auditability.

Retention and deletion should follow the applicable internal policies of the organization operating the CRM.

## 10. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes in the Custom GPT, backend workflow, data handling approach, or applicable requirements.

The latest version will be available at this URL.

## 11. Contact

For questions about this Custom GPT or its connected CRM workflow, contact the GPT owner or the internal system administrator responsible for the EYFS CRM workflow.
